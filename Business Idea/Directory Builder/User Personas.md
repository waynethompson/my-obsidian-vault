# Directory Builder — User Personas

## Overview

The Directory Builder is a multi-tenant SaaS platform. Four roles exist within each directory deployment, each with fundamentally different goals and access levels.

---

## Persona 1 — The Platform Owner (SaaS Operator)

**Who they are**
The person or team running the Directory Builder SaaS itself — managing all tenants, billing, and global settings. This is the builder of the product.

**What they want**
- Oversee all directories created on the platform
- Monitor for abuse and manage content policies
- Set pricing plans and billing
- Seed initial directories to prove the product's value

**Key frustrations**
- Tenant onboarding friction slows growth
- Abuse or spam directories dilute perceived platform quality
- Lack of visibility into which directories are active vs dormant

**Success looks like**
New directory owners onboard self-serve without support tickets. Active directories generate enough revenue per tenant to cover hosting costs within three months.

---

## Persona 2 — The Directory Owner

**Who they are**
An individual or small team who creates and manages a specific directory — e.g., "Offshoring Companies in the Philippines", "Suit Tailors in Bangkok", or "Dive Shops in Queensland". Could be an entrepreneur, a niche publisher, or a community manager. May be technically comfortable but does not want to code.

**What they want**
- Create a directory with a custom schema for their niche (fields, categories, filters)
- Import an existing list of businesses via CSV to seed listings immediately
- Invite listed businesses to claim and improve their own listings
- Enable messaging, ratings, and reviews without writing any code
- Brand the directory to look like their own product

**Key frustrations**
- Building a directory site from scratch requires a developer
- Existing solutions (WordPress plugins, Airtable embeds) break at scale or look unprofessional
- Inviting businesses individually is manual and slow
- No way to track whether invite emails were opened or acted on

**Success looks like**
They launch a directory with 200 seeded listings on a custom domain in a day, send one batch of claiming invites, and have 40% of businesses claim their listing within a month.

---

## Persona 3 — The Business / Listing Owner

**Who they are**
A business that appears in a directory — a dive shop, a suit tailor, a software vendor. They receive an invite to claim their listing or find it themselves. Non-technical. Probably found via email or a link on a niche forum.

**What they want**
- Claim ownership of their listing quickly (email or SMS verification)
- Update their information, hours, description, and photos
- See how many people have viewed or contacted them via the directory
- Respond to reviews or inquiries

**Key frustrations**
- Did not know they were listed — may be annoyed if details are incorrect
- Verification process is confusing or broken on mobile
- Cannot easily update details without creating yet another account on yet another platform
- No clear upside to claiming if the directory has low traffic

**Success looks like**
They receive an invite email, click through, verify via SMS in 30 seconds, update their listing in five minutes, and start receiving contact form submissions from genuine enquirers.

---

## Persona 4 — The End User / Directory Browser

**Who they are**
A member of the public or B2B buyer searching for something specific — a dive shop near Cairns, an offshoring partner in Manila, a trusted tailor in Bangkok. They arrived via Google search or a direct link. Mobile-first. No account required.

**What they want**
- Find what they are looking for quickly using filters and search
- See enough information to make a contact decision (location, hours, photos, reviews)
- Contact the business easily via a form — without giving up their phone number
- Trust that the listing is accurate and up to date

**Key frustrations**
- Listings with incomplete or clearly outdated information
- No way to contact the business directly through the directory
- Mobile experience that is slow or hard to use
- Too many ads or spam results cluttering the real listings

**Success looks like**
They search, filter to two or three relevant results, read one listing, and send a contact message — all within two minutes on their phone.

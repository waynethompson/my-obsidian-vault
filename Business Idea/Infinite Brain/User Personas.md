# Infinite Brain — User Personas

## Overview

Infinite Brain is an AI revenue systems company targeting small and mid-sized businesses that lose leads due to slow response, inconsistent follow-up, and manual inbox management. The primary buyers are owner-operators and operations leads in service businesses where a single lead is worth meaningful revenue.

---

## Persona 1 — The Clinic Owner (Aesthetic / Dental)

**Who they are**
Owner or practice manager of an aesthetic or dental clinic with 3–15 staff. Age 35–55. Running the business full-time. Gets 50–200 Facebook/LINE inquiries per week. Staff manually reply when they have time — which is not always fast enough.

**What they want**
- Every inquiry gets an instant, professional reply — even at 11pm
- Leads are qualified before staff time is spent on them
- Bookings are captured without a phone call
- A dashboard showing how many inquiries converted to appointments

**Key frustrations**
- Leads go cold because the first reply took four hours
- Staff waste time answering the same five questions every day
- No idea which marketing channel is generating the best leads
- Patient details are scattered across LINE, Facebook, phone notes, and paper

**Success looks like**
Response time drops from hours to seconds. Bookings increase 20–30% within 60 days. Staff spend time on confirmed appointments, not chasing inquiries.

---

## Persona 2 — The Property Agent / Agency Owner

**Who they are**
A real estate agent or small agency handling residential or commercial listings. In Thailand or SE Asia. Age 30–50. Deals arrive through Facebook, LINE, and referrals. High deal value means even one lost lead is painful.

**What they want**
- AI that qualifies leads (budget, timeline, property type) before they see them
- Automated follow-up for leads who enquired but went quiet
- Integration with their existing Line OA or Facebook Page
- Clear pipeline visibility: who has been contacted, who replied, who is serious

**Key frustrations**
- Spending hours each morning going through unread messages
- Forgetting to follow up with a prospect who was interested last week
- New agents miss messages because they are not monitoring the right channels
- No consistent data on where deals come from

**Success looks like**
They arrive at work each morning with a prioritised list of warm leads, all pre-qualified and ready for a human follow-up call. Cold leads are being nurtured automatically.

## Persona 3 — The Visa / Relocation Service Operator

**Who they are**
Owner or manager of a visa consultancy or relocation service. Age 30–50. High inquiry volume from Facebook ads and Google. Each inquiry could become a multi-thousand-dollar engagement. Currently responding manually via Facebook Messenger and LINE.

**What they want**
- Automated initial qualification: which visa, which country, what is the timeline
- Fast responses to common questions (costs, processing times, document requirements)
- Structured lead data pushed into a CRM or spreadsheet automatically
- Flagging of complex cases that need immediate human attention

**Key frustrations**
- Same questions answered 40 times per week by staff
- Genuinely urgent cases buried in a sea of information requests
- Drop-off when responses are slow during busy periods
- No way to measure inquiry volume or conversion rate reliably

**Success looks like**
A structured lead record is created for every inquiry automatically. Staff only see cases that need a human decision. The business can measure inquiry volume and conversion for the first time.

## Persona 4 — The Operations Manager / Sales Lead

**Who they are**
An operations or sales leader inside a service business — not the founder, but the person responsible for the revenue workflow and team performance. Age 28–45. Analytically minded. Currently tracking performance in spreadsheets.

**What they want**
- Visibility into the full lead lifecycle: inquiry → qualified → booked → won
- Reports that show team response time, conversion rate by channel, and revenue attributed to each source
- Automation that reduces manual data entry without replacing the human relationship
- Easy integration with existing tools (CRM, calendar, accounting)

**Key frustrations**
- Cannot produce a reliable weekly pipeline report because data lives in three places
- Response time SLAs are aspirational, not enforced
- Staff do not update the CRM consistently, so reporting is always an estimate
- Building automation in Zapier was technically possible but broke every few months

**Success looks like**
A single weekly report shows inquiry volume, response times, conversion rate, and revenue booked — pulled from real data, not manually assembled. Automation has been running without breaking for three months.

## Persona 5 — The Recruitment Agency Principal

**Who they are**
Owner or director of a small-to-mid recruitment agency. Age 35–55. Manages a team of consultants. Leads come through LinkedIn, Facebook, and referrals. High drop-off between first inquiry and booked call.

**What they want**
- Automated first response to inbound job briefs from employers
- Candidate outreach workflows that run without consultant oversight
- Lead qualification before a consultant's time is committed to a brief
- CRM records updated automatically from conversations

**Key frustrations**
- Consultants forget to follow up on candidates who applied last week
- New enquiries sit in LinkedIn message requests for 24+ hours
- CRM is always out of date because updating it feels like admin, not work
- No standard process — each consultant handles their pipeline differently

**Success looks like**
Every inbound enquiry receives a structured, professional reply within five minutes. Consultants see a prioritised list of high-intent candidates and employer briefs each morning, with CRM records already populated.

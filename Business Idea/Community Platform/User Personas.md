# Community Platform — User Personas

## Overview

The Community Platform serves Australian community groups, clubs, and scouting organisations. Five distinct personas interact with the platform, each with different goals, frustrations, and technical confidence levels.

---

## Persona 1 — The Club Admin (Volunteer Treasurer / Secretary)

**Who they are**
A volunteer who has been handed the admin responsibility for a local sports club, scouts group, or hobby organisation. They are usually mid-40s to 60s, employed full-time, and running club admin in their spare time. Technical confidence varies — they can use Facebook and spreadsheets but struggle with complex software.

**What they want**
- Replace the spreadsheet chaos (member lists, payment records, event rosters) with one system
- Send emails and SMS alerts to members without copying lists between tools
- Collect membership fees and event payments online
- Produce a simple financial summary for the committee each month

**Key frustrations**
- Members pay via bank transfer and they manually reconcile each one
- Facebook group is the "website" — no professional presence
- New committee members inherit broken spreadsheets every year
- Compliance documents (Working With Children checks) are tracked in a separate folder nobody can find

**Success looks like**
Membership renewals happen automatically, payments land in the account, and the committee actually reviews accurate figures at monthly meetings.

---

## Persona 2 — The Club Member

**Who they are**
An active participant — a parent volunteer, a sports player, or a hobbyist — who interacts with the club regularly but has no admin role. Age range 25–55. Comfortable with apps and online payments.

**What they want**
- Buy event tickets and renew membership online without chasing a treasurer
- See upcoming events and club news in one place
- Download documents (membership card, receipts)
- Optional: track their volunteer hours or badge progress

**Key frustrations**
- Has to use cash or bank transfer for everything
- Misses events because communication is scattered across Facebook, email, and group texts
- Needs to re-fill their details every time they join a new season

**Success looks like**
They renew their membership and buy an event ticket in two minutes on their phone, get a receipt instantly, and see all club news in one feed.

---

## Persona 3 — The Parent of a Junior Member

**Who they are**
A parent whose child participates in scouts, junior sports, or a youth club. They are the decision-maker and payment holder. Often juggling multiple children and activities. Age 30–50.

**What they want**
- Sign up their child quickly and securely
- Manage emergency contacts, medical info, and consents in one place
- Get timely alerts when events are on
- Understand what their child has achieved (badges, milestones)

**Key frustrations**
- Different forms are collected every year — same information, different paper
- Photo permission forms arrive at the last minute via paper note in a bag
- No visibility into what their child is working toward

**Success looks like**
They enrol their child in five minutes, their contact details carry over year to year, and they get a push notification the day before each session.

---

## Persona 4 — The Supplier (Uniform / Equipment Vendor)

**Who they are**
A B2B seller — a uniform supplier, sports equipment company, or badge manufacturer — who wants access to the network of clubs on the platform. SMB to mid-market. Has a sales or partnerships manager.

**What they want**
- List their products in front of multiple clubs in their target sport or activity category
- Offer club-specific pricing or bulk discounts without managing each relationship manually
- Earn distribution by embedding their products into clubs' own stores
- Run seasonal campaigns (e.g., back to school, new season kits)

**Key frustrations**
- Currently managing hundreds of individual club relationships by phone and email
- No visibility into which clubs bought what or when contracts lapse
- Clubs are hard to reach — no centralised procurement contact

**Success looks like**
They set up a product catalogue once, agree commission rates with the platform, and clubs can add their products to their store with one click. Orders flow through the platform without manual intervention.

---

## Persona 5 — The National Organisation Admin

**Who they are**
An employee at a peak body — Scouts Australia, a national sporting federation, or a state league. Responsible for consistency across dozens or hundreds of affiliated clubs. Age 35–55, office-based.

**What they want**
- A single dashboard showing member counts, compliance status, and event attendance across all branches
- Enforce branding and compliance requirements without micromanaging each club
- Roll out platform updates and policy changes across the network
- Generate federation-wide reports for government funding or board reporting

**Key frustrations**
- Every club uses different tools — impossible to get consistent data
- Working With Children check compliance is manually chased
- Some branches are invisible — no idea if they are active until they stop paying affiliation fees

**Success looks like**
Every affiliated club is on the platform. Compliance reports run in one click. The federation dashboard shows real-time member counts, overdue renewals, and event activity across all branches.

# Emplimate — User Personas

## Overview

Emplimate is an AI-powered niche job board platform. Multiple distinct personas interact with the system — across job seekers, employers, recruiters, and platform operators who create their own branded boards.

---

## Persona 1 — The Job Seeker

**Who they are**
A professional actively looking for work in a specific niche — e.g., an IT professional in Thailand, a defence-sector worker, or a regional job seeker in Far North Queensland. Age 22–45. Uses LinkedIn and indeed but finds them too noisy for their specific situation.

**What they want**
- Find relevant jobs in their niche without wading through hundreds of irrelevant results
- One-click apply with a saved profile and resume
- Personalised job recommendations that improve over time
- Preparation support before interviews (AI interview prep)

**Key frustrations**
- Generic job boards surface jobs from the wrong industry, location, or seniority level
- Applying to 20 jobs with the same generic form is demoralising
- No feedback on why they were not shortlisted
- Resume disappears into a black hole after applying

**Success looks like**
They find 3–5 highly relevant roles per week, apply in under two minutes each, and receive a clear recommendation matched to their actual skills — not just keywords on their resume.

---

## Persona 2 — The SME Employer / Hiring Manager

**Who they are**
A hiring manager at a small-to-mid-sized company — a Thai tech company, a regional business, or a niche industry firm. Age 30–50. Has hiring needs but no dedicated recruiter or HR team. Posts jobs on Facebook or existing boards and is overwhelmed by the volume of irrelevant applications.

**What they want**
- Post a job quickly, for free
- Receive a shortlist of genuinely qualified candidates — not 80 unscreened CVs
- Ask custom screening questions to filter candidates automatically
- Schedule interviews without email back-and-forth

**Key frustrations**
- Free job boards attract too many off-target applicants
- Reviewing every CV is time-consuming when most are clearly unsuitable
- Cannot easily compare candidates side by side
- Hiring process drags on for weeks due to coordination overhead

**Success looks like**
They post a role, set three screening questions, and within 48 hours receive an AI-ranked shortlist of 5–10 candidates with scores and explanations. They book interviews directly from the platform.

---

## Persona 3 — The Recruiter / Recruitment Agency

**Who they are**
A recruiter at a staffing agency or an independent headhunter managing multiple client roles simultaneously. Technically comfortable with ATS software. Often posting the same roles across multiple platforms.

**What they want**
- Post once and distribute to multiple niche boards that are relevant for each role
- Access candidate profiles across the Emplimate network for outreach
- Track applications, notes, and interview stages in one place per role
- White-label or agency-branded board for their niche or client

**Key frustrations**
- Currently copying and pasting job ads into 4–5 different platforms
- Candidate data is siloed — different accounts per platform
- No good way to demonstrate sourcing quality to clients
- Paying for multiple subscriptions for tools that do not talk to each other

**Success looks like**
They post a role once and distribute it to four relevant Emplimate boards. All applications flow into a single employer dashboard. They close a placement and prove their sourcing quality with a clear analytics report.

---

## Persona 4 — The Niche Board Operator

**Who they are**
A community organiser, industry association manager, or entrepreneurial individual who wants to run a job board for a specific vertical or region. Could be a developer, a trade association staffer, or an ambitious professional. Technical comfort varies.

**What they want**
- Launch a branded job board in their niche (e.g., "Defence Jobs Australia", "FNQ Local Jobs") without building from scratch
- Attract employers in their vertical through SEO and community promotion
- Earn income from featured listings or AI upsell revenue sharing
- Accept inbound cross-posted jobs from the wider Emplimate network

**Key frustrations**
- Building a job board from scratch requires engineering resources they do not have
- Getting enough listings to attract job seekers is a chicken-and-egg problem
- Existing white-label platforms are expensive and require technical setup

**Success looks like**
They launch a branded board on a custom domain in under a day, receive inbound jobs via the Emplimate network, and start earning revenue sharing from employer AI upgrades within the first month.

---

## Persona 5 — The Platform Admin (Emplimate Operator)

**Who they are**
The internal team or operator managing the Emplimate.com master platform — responsible for onboarding new board operators, managing cross-posting trust, handling abuse, and setting network pricing.

**What they want**
- Visibility into all tenants, jobs, and applications across the network
- Ability to approve or suspend board operators and enforce content policies
- Analytics showing network health: job volume, application rates, active employers
- Levers to grow the network: invite top employers to cross-post, seed listings in new boards

**Key frustrations**
- Spam or low-quality job postings that degrade the candidate experience
- Board operators who set up a board and go inactive
- Difficulty proving network value to new operators before they have listings

**Success looks like**
A clear admin dashboard with trust scoring per board, the ability to approve cross-posting flows, and network-wide metrics that support pricing decisions and investor reporting.
